CAREER

Service Design

Team project/ Sep. 2020 - Oct. 2020


This is a project for the course ‘Designing for services’ under Aalto ARTS by Tuuli Mattelmäki and Nuria Solson. Our client for the project is the City of Espoo, they aim at improving the public services with the goal of making Espoo an internationally inclusive and diverse city.

Client's brief

Our client for the project is the City of Espoo, they aim at improving the public services with the goal of making Espoo an internationally inclusive and diverse city. Client’s original brief is:

" Currently the roles and responsibilities of the student, universities, employers, and city services are unclear. How should the city and university collaborate to secure a smooth transition between career services and city employment services?

Most jobs are 'hidden', thus building a professional network is key,
and work-life connections must be built already during studies (thesis
work, internships, summer jobs)"

Reinterpret client's brief

We will create solution for not only students but also recruiters, especially small-scaled companies and emerging startups, so that the gap in hidden networks are bridged from both the ends.

User research

We faced the situation at this stage, where we've got too many insights and opportunities for people under different contexts. We found typology is a good tool to help us eliminate our target group.

Our target group is
- International students in the field of Art and Design, Business from the start of their studies
- SMEs and startups

Concept & Solution

Our proposal - CAREER (care for your career) is a unified collective of existing, enhanced, and new services that would help the international students right from the orientation week till they graduate. Resulting from the co-design workshops, the shortlisted services that would complement each other at different stages of a student in Aalto to expand their work-life contact without being additionally too demanding but curated for their unique needs. 

Here, we would like to introduce you our solution by presenting a story of a typology of student we found through research. The story depicts from the perspective of Mona, our main chararcter, an international student who starts her master studies in Design at Aalto University.
